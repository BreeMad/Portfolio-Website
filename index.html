About

Hi, and welcome to my portfolio! I am an aspiring data analyst with a passion for transforming raw data into meaningful insights that can guide better decision-making. I truly believe that data tells powerful stories, and I'm eager to dive deeper into its possibilities.

My interest in data analysis began with a love for numbers and recognizing patterns. Currently, I'm focused on sharpening my skills in data manipulation, statistical analysis, and data visualization. I enjoy exploring complex datasets to uncover trends and relationships that can impact business strategies.

I earned both my master's degree in economics and my bachelor's in quantitative analysis economics from San Diego State University. Plus, I have an associate's degree in economics with honors from Grossmont College.

When I'm not analyzing data, I love working on personal projects. I am committed to continuous learning and strive to keep up with the latest trends and technologies in the field.

I also enjoy sharing what I learn, which is why I write articles about my experiences and insights. I hope these resources can assist fellow data enthusiasts in their learning journeys.

Feel free to check out my projects and writings linked below. Don't hesitate to reach out if you want to connect or discuss anything related to data analytics!

Sales Insights: Unveiling Profitability in Retail Data

My main goal was to understand how different products were performing, spot trends in regional sales, and assess which customer segments were the most profitable to optimize business performance.

Turning 21: Analyzing the Impact of Legal Drinking Age on Hard Drug Consumption

In this replication study, I took a closer look at how the legal drinking age of 21 impacts hard drug consumption patterns among young adults using data from the 1997 National Longitudinal Survey of Youth (NLSY97). This research intended to verify the findings of the chosen empirical study by Monica Deza using the same methods and techniques.

Skills demonstrated: Stata, regression discontinuity analysis, data collection, data wrangling, statistical modeling, data visualization

Publication: Toward Data Science

The Influence of Rotten Tomatoes: Assessing the Impact of Movie Ratings on Box Office Success

In this project, I examined how Rotten Tomatoes ratings impact opening weekend box office revenues. Using a cross-sectional dataset from 2009 to 2018, I investigated the role that such ratings play in consumer decision-making within the movie industry.

Skills demonstrated: Stata, EDA, regression discountinuity analysis, data collection, data cleaning, data visualization

Understanding Electric Utility Liability in the Context of Wildfire Management

In this project, I closely examined the relationship between electric utility liability and wildfire management, with a specific focus on California's rules on inverse condemnation. By taking a closer look into how often utility-caused wildfires occur and the impact that they have, I sought to gain a better understanding of how legal frameworks shape wildfire management strategies.

Skills demonstrated: Data analysis, economic modeling, comparing situations, reviewing literature, understanding policy impacts
